About Online Data Analyst Course


Why become a Data Analyst?

Today, businesses across all industries must be data-driven to remain competitive, and they need qualified data analysts to make sense of massive and complex data sets to make better-informed decisions. Becoming a data analyst opens many high-paying, rewarding career opportunities. Professionals proficient in data analytics can apply their skills to many other job roles throughout their careers, as well. This Data Analyst training program will enable you to master descriptive and inferential statistics, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, data blending, data extraction, and forecasting to help you meet the increasing demand in this field.

Read more about how to become a data analyst here.

Data Analyst Course Learning Objectives

In this Data Analyst certification course, you will learn the latest analytics tools and techniques, how to work with SQL databases and basic SQL queries, R and Python programming, raw data manipulation, the art of creating data visualizations, and how to apply statistics and predictive analytics in a business environment.

Job Roles After Certification

Upon completing the Data Analyst certification course, you will have the data analysis skills necessary to get your dream job in the data analytics space. Apart from Data Analyst, other job titles include:

  • Data Analytics Manager/Lead

  • Business Intelligence Analyst

  • Various managerial roles

Data Analyst Course FAQs

What is Data Analytics?

Data analytics uses analytical and statistical tools and techniques to identify patterns and trends in raw data to answer questions, solve problems, predict future outcomes, and create better business strategies. The four main types of data analytics include:

  • Descriptive analytics: What happened?
  • Diagnostic analytics: Why did it happen?
  • Predictive analytics: What will happen in the future?
  • Prescriptive analytics: What can be done to ensure better outcomes?
Simplilearn’s Data Analyst Course covers all these aspects and offers a comprehensive understanding of the field, including its practical applications.
If you want a more detailed understanding of Data Analytics, this simplilearn article on What is Data Analytics will help you.

What does a data analyst do?

At a high level, data analysts:

  • Identify, gather, and import relevant data from various sources, such as databases, websites, applications, and APIs.
    This involves working with different teams to understand their problems and requirements.
  • Clean and organize data by identifying duplicate, inaccurate, incomplete, inconsistent,
    and missing data and creating structured databases to prepare it for deeper analysis. Critical tools include SQL, Excel, R, and Python.
  • Gain insights from data using statistical and machine learning tools and techniques, including regression analysis,
    cluster analysis, ANOVA tests, decision trees, predictive modeling, etc.
  • Present clear and understandable findings to key stakeholders through dashboards and
    reports using data visualization tools like Tableau and Power BI.
To learn more, check out this article about the role of a data analyst.
